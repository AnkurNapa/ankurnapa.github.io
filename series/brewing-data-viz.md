Most brewery dashboards stop at the line chart and the control chart — which answer "what's the trend" and "are we in spec" and little else. This 5-part series is about the *other* questions a brewery asks constantly, and the underused visualizations that answer them well: does this batch still taste like the brand, is a value slowly drifting, how does flavour age, and where do extract, water and energy go.

No hype, as always. Each chart earns its place against a specific brewing question — and where a plain line chart is still the right answer, I'll say so.

## The series

1. **[Beyond the Control Chart: Data Visualizations Brewers Underuse]({{ '/2026/brewing-viz-beyond-control-chart/' | relative_url }})** — the opener: matching the chart to the brewing question instead of defaulting to the line.
2. **[True-to-Target: Visualizing Whether a Batch Matches Its Brand Profile]({{ '/2026/true-to-target-batch-brand-profile-viz/' | relative_url }})** — radar profiles against a target band, deviation charts and brand-conformance trends.
3. **[SPC That Actually Catches Drift: Capability, CUSUM and EWMA]({{ '/2026/spc-capability-cusum-ewma-brewing/' | relative_url }})** — the SPC the basic control chart misses, for the slow drift brewing actually suffers.
4. **[Shelf-Life Trajectories: Visualizing Forced-Ageing and Flavour Stability]({{ '/2026/shelf-life-flavour-stability-trial-viz/' | relative_url }})** — staling as a path, with trajectories and small multiples on shared axes.
5. **[Where Your Extract, Water and Energy Go: Sankey and the Brewhouse Balance]({{ '/2026/sankey-brewhouse-mass-energy-balance/' | relative_url }})** — the whole mass and energy balance in one picture, with the widest loss band pointing where to act.
