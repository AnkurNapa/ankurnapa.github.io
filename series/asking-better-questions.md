Everyone *wants* to use AI. Almost nobody is taught how to use it thoughtfully — and for brewers early in their journey, the hardest part isn't the tool, it's that they don't yet have the experience to know **what to ask**. This 5-part series builds that skill deliberately: not prompt tricks, but question literacy.

No hype, as always. Where AI helps, I'll show you. Where it confidently lies, I'll show you that too.

## The series

1. **[You Don't Have an AI Problem, You Have a Question Problem]({{ '/2026/ai-question-problem-new-brewers/' | relative_url }})** — why beginners get generic mush, and what a knowable question looks like.
2. **[The Beginner Brewer's Cheat Code: Ask AI What You Should Be Asking]({{ '/2026/ask-ai-what-to-ask-brewing/' | relative_url }})** — flip the tool: questions are cheap to verify, answers are expensive to get wrong.
3. **[A Brewer's Map of Their Own Data]({{ '/2026/brewery-data-map-what-it-answers/' | relative_url }})** — five piles of data you already have, and what each can actually answer.
4. **[Ten Prompts from the Brewery Floor: Bad Ask → Good Ask]({{ '/2026/brewery-floor-prompts-bad-to-good/' | relative_url }})** — real situations rewritten: style + stage + numbers + timeline + one decision.
5. **[How to Tell a Good AI Answer from a Confidently Wrong One]({{ '/2026/spotting-confident-wrong-ai-answers-brewing/' | relative_url }})** — the claim-sorting discipline that makes the rest of the series safe.

Read them in order — each builds on the last. Every post in the series is tagged [#asking-better-questions]({{ '/tags/' | relative_url }}#asking-better-questions).

If you're further along the journey, the companion series [From Brewer to AI]({{ '/series/brewer-to-ai/' | relative_url }}) tells the longer story — from the brewhouse to data science, including where AI burned me.
