Everyone tells brewers to "get into AI" and almost nobody shows them the actual steps. This 4-part series is the map and the reading list: a staged, practical roadmap from the brewhouse to working AI, with a specific free, open resource behind every step — Microsoft Learn, Kaggle Learn, freeCodeCamp, Google's Machine Learning Crash Course, and Google's PAIR People + AI Guidebook. The only thing it costs is consistent evenings.

This is the *how-to* companion to my first-person [From Brewer to AI]({{ '/series/brewer-to-ai/' | relative_url }}) story. No hype, as always — where each tool helps, I'll show you; where it confidently misleads, I'll show you that too.

## The series

1. **[A Brewer's Step-by-Step Roadmap into AI (with Free Resources)]({{ '/2026/brewer-ai-roadmap-step-by-step/' | relative_url }})** — the five stages, why you already own the hardest part, and the whole free toolkit at a glance.
2. **[Foundations First: Data Literacy and Python for Brewers]({{ '/2026/brewer-ai-foundations-data-python-free-resources/' | relative_url }})** — spreadsheets to Power BI with Microsoft Learn, then just-enough Python with Kaggle Learn and freeCodeCamp.
3. **[Your First Machine-Learning Model on Brewing Data]({{ '/2026/brewer-first-machine-learning-model-free-courses/' | relative_url }})** — the concepts free via Google's ML Crash Course and Kaggle, then a small real model in scikit-learn, judged honestly.
4. **[Generative and Human-Centred AI for Brewers]({{ '/2026/brewer-generative-human-centered-ai-pair-guidebook/' | relative_url }})** — using generative AI well, and building with it responsibly using Google's PAIR People + AI Guidebook.

Read them in order — each builds on the last. Every post in the series is tagged [#brewer-ai-roadmap]({{ '/tags/' | relative_url }}#brewer-ai-roadmap).

When you're ready to apply it, the [Brewing Science &amp; AI track]({{ '/tracks/brewing-science-ai/' | relative_url }}) is full of concrete brewing problems to learn against.
