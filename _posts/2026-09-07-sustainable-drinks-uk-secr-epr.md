**Short answer: UK drinks producers face Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR), extended producer responsibility (EPR) for packaging, and the plastic packaging tax. The data work is the same as anywhere — meter energy, weigh packaging — mapped to UK rules. AI assembles the figures; generative AI drafts the filings.**

The UK's regime rewards producers who already measure: SECR wants energy and carbon, EPR wants packaging data by material and weight. Both are data exercises before they are compliance ones.

## Measure first, model second

SECR needs energy use and emissions; EPR needs packaging placed on market by material and weight. Producers who sub-meter and track packaging per SKU already hold most of what both require.

## Where AI and data cut UK sustainability compliance

Data engineering consolidates energy and packaging data and applies UK emission factors; analytics model EPR fees under different packaging mixes so the commercial choice is informed.

## Where generative AI (Claude, ChatGPT) helps

A copilot maps your data to the SECR narrative and EPR submission and drafts both, citing your verified figures. The rule holds: it drafts and explains, a person verifies anything that reaches a regulator.

## Where it breaks

UK rules and thresholds change, and EPR in particular has been phasing in with shifting detail — treat this as orientation, not legal advice, and confirm obligations for your size and packaging with a specialist.

## The bottom line

UK sustainability compliance rewards measurement: meter energy for SECR, weigh packaging for EPR, and let AI assemble and generative AI draft. The data you need is mostly data you should already keep.
