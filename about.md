**Brews & Bytes is an independent blog about where beer meets artificial intelligence.** It covers fermentation forecasting, brewing analytics, AI-assisted recipe design, sensory/tasting models, and the data science that helps brewers make better beer.

## What you'll find here

- **Fermentation & process** — how machine learning models predict attenuation, timing, and off-flavors.
- **Brewing analytics** — what data is worth collecting, and what to do with it.
- **AI in the taproom** — demand forecasting, DTC, and customer insight for breweries.
- **Plain-English explainers** — practitioner-focused, no hype.

## Privacy

This is a personal blog, and I try to respect your privacy.

**What's collected.** To understand which posts resonate, this site uses
privacy-conscious analytics that record **anonymous, aggregate** data only:
approximate location (country/city, derived from IP — never the full IP),
the date and time of a visit, which pages were viewed, how visitors arrived
(for example from a search engine or a shared link), and general device and
browser type.

**What's *not* collected.** I do not collect your name, email, or any
information that personally identifies you, and I never see who you individually
are. I do not sell, share, or trade any data, and there are no ads or
third-party advertising trackers.

**Cookies.** Depending on the analytics provider in use, a small first-party
analytics cookie may be set to tell new visits from returning ones. It is used
only for aggregate statistics. You can block or delete cookies anytime in your
browser settings, and the site will work exactly the same.

**Your choices.** You can opt out by enabling "Do Not Track" or Global Privacy
Control in your browser, using a tracker-blocking extension, or browsing in
private/incognito mode. Any of these stops the analytics from recording your
visit.

**Questions or requests.** If you'd like to ask about this data or request its
removal, open an issue on the [GitHub repository]({{ site.url }}).
